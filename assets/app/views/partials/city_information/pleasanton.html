Pleasanton at a glance

When looking for a place to live or spend a pleasant afternoon, it is difficult to choose a better location than Pleasanton. When 'Pleasant' is built right into the name, the bar is set high, and Pleasanton delivers on that promise.

Location

Situated near the junction of the i580 and i680 corridors in the East Bay of San Francisco, Pleasanton is a great location for commuters working in either Silicon Valley or San Francisco.

Transit

When your home town abuts two major interstate arteries, you know that you have some pleasant transit options at your disposal. In addition to the exceptional freeway access, Pleasanton has a wealth of transit options for you to choose from, including:

  • BART - Bay Area Rapid Transit
  • ACE Train (Altamont Commuter Express)
  • Caltrans and Amtrak
  • Wheels and other similar local transit hub options

Beyond the freeways and mass transit lies a well thought out, and well maintained infrastructure of service arteries to service the robust local shopping and bustling business district.

Industry

While ideally situated for commuters, Pleasanton has a rich local economy and is far from being a 'bedroom community.' The city has at least 5 employers with over a thousand local employees, ranging from Oracle Campus to the national Safeway headquarters.

Public Services

With its 'just right' community size, stable tax base and low unemployment rates, Pleasanton provides attractive public services to its residents and visitors. In addition it offers exceptional shopping options ranging from the lovely StoneRidge Mall to a busy yet accessible downtown experience. When the sun goes low in the Western sky, the nightlife heats up, meaning that no matter the time of day, the city has something interesting and fun for you to do.

Schools

With nearly 80 total schools, Pleasanton schools rank in the 90th percentile overall based on testing data. In addition there is a strong compliment of public and privately funded activities from hobbies and crafts to sports to engage future generations in constructive activities.
