Hayward at a glance

Hayward is a large suburb sitting directly on the San Francisco Bay near to the San Mateo - Hayward bridge that transports nearly a quarter of the daily traffic from the East Bay to the peninsula. As of the 2010 Census it is nearly equally split between owner occupied and rental properties. Encompassing a massive space compared to its population, Hayward boasts miles of undeveloped marsh waterfront, which is home to countless species of fish and fowl.

Location

Like its neighbors in Union City and San Leandro, Hayward boasts a great location in the East Bay, midway between San Jose/Silicon Valley and San Francisco. It is literally referred to as the 'heart of the Bay Area' for its easy access to San Jose, San Francisco, and just about every other part of the Bay Area. With some of the largest open space reserves in the area, Hayward is a great location to take in everything that the Bay Area has to offer. The city has come a long way in the last 25 years according to most long time residents. If the city adapts itself to better take advantage of its strategic position in the East Bay, current property values would be a tremendous bargain.

Transit

Hayward directly over the i880 North-South freeway. It is just north of I-84 (Dunbarton Bridge), and bisected by I-92, which leads to the San Mateo Bridge. . Given its location, both the San Mateo and the Dunbarton Bridge are an option to cross the Bay. City streets are covered by AC Transit Bus services, BART light rail service, and the nationally connected Amtrak train service.

Industry

Kaiser, CSU East Bay, Chabot College and Berkeley Farms are just a few of the top employers to a community that nearly 4,000 businesses call home.

Schools

Hayward, while it has some well rated schools, is in the 30th percentile for test scores. However, some of the most challenged areas recently received 25 million dollars in grant funding from the Department of Education's Promise Neighborhood Program, which will fund a variety of initiatives over the next 5 years. The city is also home to CSU and Chabot College.
