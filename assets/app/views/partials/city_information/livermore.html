Livermore at a glance

Historic Livermore has even more to offer today as the metro areas of San Jose and San Francisco are brought closer to this once rural location via transit and technology. Offering a pleasant mix of modern and country feel, Livermore is a unique northern Californian treasure. Famous for the massive Lawrence Livermore Laboratory, the city is also known for its mix of classic and contemporary housing, vineyards and growing wine industry, and excellent eclectic dining.

Location

Livermore marks the eastern end of the trivalley area, as the valley floor climbs steeply to the wind farms and rolling hills of the Altamont. As the eastern side of the Altamont slopes down in turn, Alameda county gives way to San Joaquin county and the last bastions of true urban living in Northern California. West of this range, the Livermore valley has greeted millions of land travellers to the region over the last few centuries, giving them their first peek at the last valley before the bay on the other side of the Berkeley and Oakland hills.

Much likes its wines, Livermore presents itself with delightfully crisp views of the trivalley area, with a smooth and subtle finish that is the result of a diverse yet accessible culture.

Transit

Recent freeway expansions on the East/West i580 artery have increased capacity and convenience by bringing more lanes and HOV options. While the BART system does not extend to Livermore, it is only a hop away in neighboring Dublin/Pleasanton. Moreoever, Livermore benefits from the i84 route connecting the 580 to the 680 southbound, serving numerous commuters headed to the Silicon Valley. You might still find a sign of the Old West here and there in the form of a stage coach, but the modern commuter has access to good options to travel to or from the Livermore valley.

Industry

Originally the product of the somewhat isolated Lawrence Livermore Lab research facility, the city has grown to fill out its corporate footprint in recent decades. Significant employers after the massive laboratory include Comcast Cable, Valley Care Health and Wente Vineyards.

Public Services

Downtown Livermore is one of those delightful, walkable artifacts from the past. With the completely authentic Old West 'Main Street' feeling, but populated with modern and contemporary businesses, historic downtown Livermore is a must see.

Schools

With approximately 125 total schools, Livermore schools are positioned in the upper 80th percentile overall based on testing data. Before and after school activities cover a spectrum as broad as the city and its residents.
