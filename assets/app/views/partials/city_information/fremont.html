Fremont at a glance

Fremont is the largest of San Francisco's suburbs, and among the most populous. The city has a rich historical pedigree, and unique character given its fairly recent formation from the consolidation of five existing communities in Centerville, Niles, Irvington, Mission San Jose and Warm Springs Today the sprawling suburban area merges together with Newark and Union City, and is associated equally with the San Francisco Peninsula, but also San Jose and Silicon Valley.

Location

Just north of the greater San Jose metropolitan area, and just across the water from San Francisco, Fremont is an ideal location with beautiful views in all directions. As such, it offers exceptional access to all that Northern California has to offer while still being accessible to the average family. Open spaces and lakes dot the landscape, offering ample outdoor activity options.

Transit

Fremont sits between the i680 and i880 North-South freeways, and just between highways 84 and 237 which connect it with the peninsula and San Jose. With east access to BART, Amtrak and the ACE trains, as well a both AC Transit (East Bay) and the VTA (Santa Clara County), Fremont has excellent transit options.

Industry

Fremont, with nearly a quarter of a million residents, has a robust local economy, with long standing corporate residents like Association Management Solutions headquartered there. In addition, recent initiatives have seen a surge in manufacturing in the area, as the NUMII plant transitioned to the high profile TESLA manufacturing headquarters.

Public Services

With its rich and diverse populace, and public commitment to the arts and museums, Fremont has a high profile public face as a community.

Schools

Fremont is in the 80th percentile for standardized school scores, but with almost 300 total schools, there is a wide range of scholastic options.
